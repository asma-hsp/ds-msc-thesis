\chapter{Experimental Results}\label{chapt:results}

This chapter reports and discusses the experiments that evaluate the proposed
pipeline. Section~\ref{sect:res_setup} describes the experimental setup.
Section~\ref{sect:res_main} reports the main result -- the \textsc{Basic} duplet
versus our triplet -- across all four backbones on the full i-CIR benchmark.
Section~\ref{sect:res_components} then dissects the pipeline: it isolates the
contribution of each component on a cumulative ladder
(Section~\ref{subsect:res_ladder}), studies the effect of feature centering and
the remaining \textsc{Basic} post-processing
(Section~\ref{sect:res_centering}), compares the five caption-generation
instructions (Section~\ref{subsect:res_ablation_instr}), and quantifies the
benefit of embedding-space averaging
(Section~\ref{subsect:res_aug}). Section~\ref{sect:res_backbones} analyses the
backbones and establishes the best result attainable.
Section~\ref{sect:res_qual} presents a qualitative and failure analysis, and
Section~\ref{sect:res_summary} summarises the findings.

% =====================================================================
\section{Experimental Setup}\label{sect:res_setup}
% =====================================================================

\paragraph{Data and protocol.} All results are reported on the full i-CIR
benchmark~\cite{psomas2025icir}: $1{,}883$ queries ranked against
$N = 752{,}092$ database images, under the instance-pool retrieval protocol of
Section~\ref{sect:method_eval}. Every number in this chapter is computed at full
scale.

\paragraph{Metrics.} Unless stated otherwise, all numbers are macro-mAP
(\textsc{mmAP}, \%), the primary metric of Section~\ref{sect:method_eval}
(Equation~\eqref{eq:macro_map}); micro-mAP (\textsc{mAP}) is reported alongside
where informative.

\paragraph{Configurations.} We compare the \textsc{Basic} duplet
(image$\times$text, Equation~\eqref{eq:duplet}) against \emph{Ours}, the
clamped triplet image$\times$text$\times$caption of
Equation~\eqref{eq:triplet}. The caption signal uses the \texttt{avg-5} setting
(Equation~\eqref{eq:avg5}) by default; ``Ours~[best instr.]'' denotes the
triplet using the single best-performing instruction of
Table~\ref{tab:instructions} instead of \texttt{avg-5}. Feature centering and the
\textsc{Basic} post-processing of Section~\ref{sect:method_basic} are applied as
a cumulative ladder, analysed in Section~\ref{sect:res_components}.

% =====================================================================
\section{Main Results: \textsc{Basic} Duplet vs.\ Triplet}\label{sect:res_main}
% =====================================================================

Table~\ref{tab:res_main} reports the headline result. Adding the generated
target caption improves \emph{every} backbone, without exception. The gains are
largest for the backbones whose plain image--text alignment is weakest:
SigLIP-L improves by $+22.2$ macro-mAP and SigLIP2 by $+18.0$, while the
already-strong CLIP-H and the smaller CLIP-L improve by $+6.0$ and $+7.5$
respectively. The best configuration in this table is SigLIP2 with the
\texttt{avg-5} caption, at $56.09$ macro-mAP.

The pattern -- larger gains where the duplet baseline is weaker -- is consistent
with the role of the caption. A backbone with poorly aligned text and image
spaces produces a noisy modification-text signal $\st$; the self-contained
target caption $\sc$ supplies a cleaner, image-like text query and therefore
helps such backbones the most. Where the backbone is already well aligned
(CLIP-H), the caption still helps, but the marginal benefit is smaller because
the text branch was already informative. On every backbone the \texttt{avg-5}
caption also outperforms the best single instruction, anticipating the
averaging analysis of Section~\ref{subsect:res_aug}.

\section{Component Analysis}\label{sect:res_components}
% =====================================================================

This section isolates the contribution of each component of the pipeline. We
work on the best backbone, SigLIP2, where the full \textsc{Basic} post-processing
stack of Section~\ref{sect:method_basic} can be layered on top of the triplet.

\subsection{Building the Pipeline: a Component Ladder}\label{subsect:res_ladder}

Table~\ref{tab:res_ladder} builds the pipeline up one component at a time, as a
cumulative ladder, for three configurations that share the \emph{exact same}
post-processing so the caption is the only isolated variable: \textsc{Basic}
(no caption), Ours~[\texttt{avg-5}], and Ours~[\textsc{H}] (the best single
instruction for SigLIP2). The first row is the plain product score; each
subsequent row adds one preprocessing step -- feature centering, similarity
min-normalisation, the Harris-style fusion penalty, query
contextualisation, the contrastive projection, and query
expansion.\footnote{SigLIP2 has no synthetic calibration set of the kind used to
fit the original \textsc{Basic} statistics for CLIP; the min-normalisation
constants are therefore estimated empirically from a database sample, and
centering uses the global database mean (Section~\ref{subsect:method_centering}).}

The single largest jump comes from the \textbf{caption}. At the product level,
adding the \texttt{avg-5} caption raises macro-mAP from $38.12$ (\textsc{Basic})
to $56.09$ -- a $+17.97$ gain that is larger than the entire post-processing
stack contributes on top of it. This is the core empirical message of the thesis:
\emph{a single generated target caption injects the textual signal more
effectively than the multi-step \textsc{Basic} preprocessing it is designed to
replace}, and it does so as a simple additive branch. The two ideas are
nonetheless complementary -- centering and contextualisation lift Ours a further
$+5.9$ to the ceiling of $61.98$ -- but the caption is unambiguously the dominant
component.

\subsection{Feature Centering and Post-Processing}\label{sect:res_centering}

Reading Table~\ref{tab:res_ladder} down the rungs reveals which parts of the
\textsc{Basic} stack actually help once the caption is present.
\textbf{Centering} is the second most important component after the caption: for
the \textsc{Basic} duplet it alone lifts macro-mAP from $38.12$ to $49.96$
($+11.8$), confirming a strong embedding-space bias in SigLIP2 that subtracting
the mean removes. \textbf{Contextualisation} is the other clear winner, providing
the largest single post-processing jump for every configuration (e.g.\ Ours rises
from $55.87$ to $61.98$). The intermediate steps are minor: min-normalisation
slightly \emph{lowers} the score before the Harris fusion penalty recovers it,
so the two roughly cancel.

The most striking observation is that the two final \textsc{Basic} steps --
the contrastive \textbf{projection} (Equation~\eqref{eq:basic_proj}) and
\textbf{query expansion} -- \emph{hurt} every configuration on SigLIP2: Ours
falls from its $61.98$ peak to $59.02$ and then $57.29$. These two steps were
designed and tuned for CLIP and do not transfer to the SigLIP2 embedding space.
The practical consequence is that the optimal pipeline on the best backbone
stops at contextualisation; the elaborate tail of the \textsc{Basic} stack can be
discarded.

\paragraph{Source of the centering mean.} A natural question
(Section~\ref{subsect:method_centering}) is whether the \emph{source} of the
mean matters. We find it does not: centering with the global database mean and
with a generic LAION-1M mean differ by at most $\sim\!0.55$ macro-mAP, and
usually by less. The choice of centering corpus is therefore not a material
hyperparameter, and we use the global database mean throughout.

\subsection{Caption Instruction Variants}\label{subsect:res_ablation_instr}

Table~\ref{tab:res_instr} compares the five instruction variants of
Section~\ref{sect:method_caption} (Table~\ref{tab:instructions}) on the SigLIP2
triplet. The best single instruction is \textsc{H} (\emph{anti-noise contract},
$53.91$), narrowly ahead of \textsc{G} (\emph{constraint-priority}, $53.70$) and
\textsc{J} (\emph{draft--refine}, $52.88$); the slot-fill template \textsc{I}
trails at $51.01$. The spread of $\sim\!2.9$ points between the best and worst
instruction shows that prompt design genuinely matters, but also that all five
variants are individually strong -- each comfortably exceeds the $38.11$ duplet
baseline. The ordering is consistent with the design rationale of
Section~\ref{sect:method_caption}: the variants that most aggressively suppress
scene and aesthetic noise (\textsc{H}, \textsc{G}) lead, while the rigid template
(\textsc{I}), which trades expressiveness for structure, is the weakest.

\subsection{Caption Augmentation: \texttt{avg-5} vs.\ Single Instructions}\label{subsect:res_aug}

Rather than commit to one prompt, the default caption embedding averages all
five instructions in embedding space (\texttt{avg-5},
Equation~\eqref{eq:avg5}). Table~\ref{tab:res_aug} shows this is a consistent
improvement: on \emph{every} backbone, \texttt{avg-5} exceeds both the best
single instruction and a selective average of the best four. The gain over the
best single instruction is small but reliable ($+0.9$ to $+2.2$ macro-mAP), and
crucially it is obtained \emph{without selecting} anything on the evaluation set
-- \texttt{avg-5} uses all five prompts unconditionally, whereas ``best
instruction'' and ``best-4'' require an oracle choice. Averaging the embeddings
keeps the signal that is consistent across instructions and attenuates the
idiosyncrasies of any single prompt, which is why we adopt it as the default.

\section{Backbone Sensitivity and the Performance Ceiling}\label{sect:res_backbones}
% =====================================================================

Table~\ref{tab:res_backbones} ranks the backbones on the bare duplet
(image$\times$text) baseline, isolating the raw quality of each encoder before
the caption is added. On the duplet, CLIP-H is the strongest backbone
($41.10$ macro-mAP), ahead of SigLIP2 ($38.11$), with SigLIP-L and CLIP-L lower.

It is instructive to contrast this duplet ranking with the triplet ranking of
Section~\ref{sect:res_main}, where \emph{SigLIP2} is best. The ordering changes
because the caption interacts differently with each encoder: SigLIP2 gains
$+18.0$ from the caption against CLIP-H's $+6.0$, so a backbone that trails on the
bare duplet leads once the third modality is added. This is precisely why a
backbone must be evaluated \emph{within} the full pipeline rather than on the
duplet baseline alone.

\subsection{Failure Analysis}\label{subsect:res_failure}

% HIGHLIGHT: build the per-category breakdown from per_query_ap.npy joined to the
% query metadata (instance category), and pick 3-5 low-AP examples per mode.
Examining the lowest-AP queries reveals three recurring failure modes, which
mirror the caption-design trade-offs identified in
Section~\ref{sect:method_caption}.
\textbf{(i) Sparse captions for unrecognised instances.} When the MLLM cannot
identify the specific instance, it falls back to a generic description (e.g.\
collapsing a particular bridge to ``a bridge''), and the caption loses the
discriminative detail needed to separate the correct instance from look-alikes.
\textbf{(ii) Residual scene and aesthetic noise.} When the caption admits
background, lighting, or mood terms, the text query drifts toward visually
similar but semantically irrelevant images.
\textbf{(iii) Lost instance identity under constraint decomposition.} When the
modification is decomposed into atomic constraints, the caption occasionally
satisfies the edit while under-specifying the instance, retrieving the right
modification on the wrong object.

\section{Summary}\label{sect:res_summary}
% =====================================================================

The experiments support the central claim of the thesis. The generated target
caption is the single most effective component of the pipeline: it improves every
backbone (Table~\ref{tab:res_main}), contributes a larger gain than the entire
\textsc{Basic} post-processing stack it replaces (Table~\ref{tab:res_ladder}),
and does so as a simple additive branch that needs no training. Embedding-space
averaging over the five instructions (\texttt{avg-5}) is a small but reliable,
selection-free improvement over any single prompt
(Table~\ref{tab:res_aug}). The best backbone within the full pipeline is SigLIP2,
not the strongest bare-duplet encoder (Section~\ref{sect:res_backbones}), and the
best result of the thesis -- $61.98$ macro-mAP -- combines the caption with the
two post-processing steps that transfer to SigLIP2, with the CLIP-specific
projection and query expansion discarded. The residual failures trace back to
caption quality rather than to the retrieval mechanism
(Section~\ref{subsect:res_failure}).
